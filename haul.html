01 · Why I built it

An ambitious bet that taught me when to pivot.

Haul began as my big swing: a social platform for fashion creators, somewhere between Pinterest and TikTok. I believed in it, designed it fully, and shipped it. The traction didn't come.

So I did the hard thing and pivoted to the utility hiding underneath: instead of a feed, just point Haul at any outfit and let AI break it down. The real lesson wasn't the model. It was learning to read the market and kill my favourite idea when it was time.

02 · How it works

Scan a whole look, shop every piece.

  • Share or snap any outfit from anywhere, including straight from another app through the Share Extension.
  • AI detects every piece at once (top, trousers, bag, shoes), each with its own bounding box, not one item at a time.
  • Each piece is matched to real products with prices across 40,000+ retailers.
  • You browse, save, and buy in one place, with no item-by-item searching.
03 · The vision

Not just Google Lens. A taste engine.

Google Lens reads one item at a time. Haul reads the whole look in a single pass, with per-item detection and real product matches. That is the wedge.

The bigger idea: every scan teaches Haul a little more about what you actually wear and save. Enough scans and it maps your taste better than any feed, which turns Haul into a personal fashion platform built on discovery and recommendation, not search. Multi-item scanning is the wedge; knowing your style is the moat.

04 · What I learned

Owning the whole thing, end to end.

Haul is where I learned to take an idea from a pitch deck to a live product to its first users: product strategy, UI/UX, Swift, backend, the AI pipeline, and the organic marketing that ran across three TikTok accounts with zero ad budget. It also taught me the most useful founder skill of all, knowing when an idea has to change.
