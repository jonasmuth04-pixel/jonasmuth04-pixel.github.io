01 · Why I built it

A real problem from my own nights out.

The idea came from a pain point I lived. Every time I went out with friends at uni, we'd have almost no photos the next day, because everyone is too lazy to actually send them around. The memories were there, just scattered across ten camera rolls.

So I built the fix: a shared album that fills itself while the night is happening, so nobody has to ask for photos ever again.

02 · How it works

One event, one live album.

  • Create an event with a date, then invite friends with a link or a QR code.
  • Everyone shoots as usual. Photos and videos sync into one shared album automatically.
  • The live function means the album fills up in real time as the night happens, not the morning after.
  • Everything stays in one place, so the next day every memory is already collected.
03 · Building with AI

My first app, and my first lesson in AI-built products.

Momio was the project where I taught myself to ship with tools like Claude Code. It was as much about learning the craft as the product: where AI genuinely speeds you up, and where you still have to own the product thinking yourself.

It was also my playground for real-time infrastructure and for testing whether organic TikTok could pull installs on a budget of exactly zero.

04 · What I learned

How to take something from idea to App Store.

Starting from a personal frustration, Momio taught me the full loop: scoping an MVP, designing it, building it with AI, wiring up a real-time backend, shipping to the App Store, and finding the first users. Every app after this one was faster because of it.
