01 · Why I built it

I was the target user.

CodeFocus was a spontaneous build while Haul was running. I kept catching myself reaching for my phone every time my coding agents were busy. The AI was doing the work, and I was scrolling.

So I made my own blocker to fix exactly that, then realised other developers had the same problem and shipped it.

02 · How it works

Distractions unlock only while your AI works.

  • Your distracting apps stay locked by default: TikTok, Instagram, X, whatever eats your day.
  • They unlock only while an AI agent is running on your Mac, like Claude Code, Codex, or Cursor.
  • The moment the agent stops, they lock again, so the break ends when the work does.
  • A Mac-to-iPhone Bluetooth link and Apple's Screen Time API make the whole thing work in real time.
03 · Shipping a real product

From idea to a monetized app.

This is the most technical thing I've shipped, and the most complete as a product. I didn't just build it: I put it on the App Store, added a subscription, and ran the marketing myself. It turned a personal annoyance into a small, real product with real users.

04 · What I learned

Building for myself is the fastest way to build something true.

CodeFocus proved I can spot a problem I personally feel, build a genuinely technical solution around it (Bluetooth, system-level Screen Time control), and take it all the way to a monetized App Store listing. Scratching my own itch made every product decision obvious.
