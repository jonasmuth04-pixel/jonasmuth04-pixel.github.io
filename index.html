01 · How I got here

I studied business, then spent my internships doing growth at startups. But I kept wanting to be closer to the product itself.

At Mannheim, Germany's #1 for business, and in growth roles at startups like Tacto and Knowunity, I learned the operator side: GTM, account-based marketing, and UGC. At Knowunity I launched the US market with a micro-creator pipeline that hit 25M+ views and 50,000+ signups in three months. That is where I learned how virality really works.

So when Claude and Cursor made building real, I went all in: six months to learn the whole pipeline, from design and Swift to backend and the App Store. The twist: at Knowunity I had a budget, on my own apps I have none, so I lean entirely on the virality playbook instead of ad spend.

It started with an ambitious bet that taught me when to pivot, then two more apps across different niches. Now I want both halves in one role: the growth operator and the builder, at an early-stage startup.
