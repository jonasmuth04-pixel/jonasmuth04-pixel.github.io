APP 01
Shopping · AI vision · The big bet

Haul AI

See it. Scan it. Shop it.

Haul began as my ambitious bet: a social platform for fashion creators. The traction didn't come, so I pivoted to the utility underneath it: point it at any outfit and AI breaks the whole look down in one pass.

That's the part that isn't just Google Lens. Lens reads one item at a time; Haul detects every piece in a full outfit (top, trousers, bag, shoes) at once, with per-item bounding boxes, then matches each to real products across 40,000+ retailers.

Where it's heading: every scan teaches Haul a little more about what you actually wear and save. Enough scans and it maps your taste better than any feed, and that taste graph turns Haul into a personal fashion platform built on discovery and recommendation, not search. Multi-item scanning is the wedge; knowing your style is the moat.

This is the project that's had my full attention for the last six months, including the marketing.

APP 02
Photo & Video · Real-time · My first build

Momio

Every photo. Every guest. One album.

The idea came from a real pain point. Every time I went out with friends at uni, we'd have almost no photos the next day, because everyone's too lazy to share. So I built the live function: create an event, share a link or QR, and every guest's photos and videos land in one shared album as the night happens.

Momio was also my first app, the project where I learned what actually matters when you build with AI like Claude Code: where it speeds you up, and where you still have to own the product thinking yourself.

APP 03
Productivity · Developer tool · Shipped & monetized

CodeFocus

Your feeds stay locked until your AI is working.

The anti-distraction app for developers who'd rather their AI did the work. TikTok, Instagram, X, and whatever else eats your day stay blocked. They only unlock while an AI coding agent like Claude Code, Codex or Cursor is actively running on your machine.

It was a spontaneous build while Haul was running. I kept catching myself reaching for my phone every time my agents were busy, so I made my own blocker, then shipped it to the App Store, put a subscription on it, and ran the marketing myself.

Built around a Mac-to-iPhone Bluetooth link and Apple's Screen Time API. The most technical thing I've shipped.
